Features of HD 209458b:

  • Known as "Osiris," it's the first exoplanet discovered transiting its star.
  • Located approximately 159 light-years from Earth in the constellation Pegasus.
  • Hot Jupiter with a short orbital period of about 3.5 days.
  • Atmospheric studies revealed evidence of sodium and carbon-containing compounds.
  • Potentially has high temperatures, making it an interesting case for exoplanet research.

Description:

HD 209458b, often referred to as Osiris, made history as the first exoplanet discovered transiting its star, allowing scientists to gather significant atmospheric data. This gas giant orbits very close to its host star, leading to extreme temperatures and fascinating atmospheric conditions.
