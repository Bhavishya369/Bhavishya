Features of HAT-P-7b:

  • Located approximately 1,040 light-years away in the constellation Cygnus.
  • Has a highly eccentric orbit with an orbital period of about 2.2 days.
  • Known for its extreme atmospheric conditions, including potential storms.
  • Discovered in 2008, it is classified as a "hot Jupiter."
  • Experiences significant tidal forces due to its close proximity to its star.

HAT-P-7b: A Planet of Extreme Conditions!

HAT-P-7b is a fascinating gas giant that orbits very close to its host star, resulting in extreme temperatures and unpredictable weather patterns. This hot Jupiter presents a unique opportunity for scientific exploration.
